# Summary

Applies the curse-of-knowledge bias to business communication: experts speak in abstract, compressed language that frontline people cannot decode, and the cure is concrete language and examples. Popularized Elizabeth Newton's tapper-and-listener finding (120 songs, 2.5 percent identified versus the roughly 50 percent tappers predicted).
