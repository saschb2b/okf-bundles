# Ticket writing and slicing

Agent-readable knowledge for writing and slicing work items: how to detect a bad ticket before pulling it, why each problem hurts, and the techniques that cure it. Synthesized from primary agile and cognitive-science sources (see references).

Start here: [Overview](overview.md).

# Smells

- [The Iceberg](smells/iceberg.md) - Looks small, hides most of its work below the waterline.
- [The Siamese Twins](smells/siamese-twins.md) - Split on paper, technically inseparable.
- [The Tapper](smells/tapper.md) - Written by one expert, decodable by no one.
- [The Boulder](smells/boulder.md) - Too big to finish; stalls on the board.

# Concepts

- [Planning fallacy](concepts/planning-fallacy.md) - We underestimate our own tasks.
- [Cone of Uncertainty](concepts/cone-of-uncertainty.md) - Early estimates are least reliable.
- [Curse of knowledge](concepts/curse-of-knowledge.md) - Knowing makes you unable to imagine not knowing.
- [Flow metrics](concepts/flow-metrics.md) - Cycle time, throughput, WIP, work item age.
- [Little's Law](concepts/littles-law.md) - Cycle time equals WIP divided by throughput.
- [Batch size](concepts/batch-size.md) - Smaller batches finish faster.
