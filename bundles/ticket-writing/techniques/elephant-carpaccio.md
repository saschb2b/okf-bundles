# What it is

A facilitated drill: take one feature and force it into the thinnest vertical slices you can, each demoable, each targeting a few hours of work. Invented by Alistair Cockburn; the widely used facilitation guide is by Henrik Kniberg.

# The reframe

Cockburn notes the name is backwards. You are not carving slices off a finished elephant; you are constructing the elephant slice by slice (armature, then walking skeleton, then a moving elephant). Slicing is incremental construction of value, not decomposition of a finished thing.

# Use it to

Build the team muscle for [vertical slicing](/techniques/vertical-slicing.md).
