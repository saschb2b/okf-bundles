# Summary

Named in a 1989 economics paper (Camerer, Loewenstein, and Weber; the phrase was suggested by Robin Hogarth) and popularized by Chip and Dan Heath in "Made to Stick". The famous demonstration is Elizabeth Newton's 1990 tapper-and-listener study: tappers of 120 well-known songs predicted listeners would identify about 50 percent; listeners got 3 (2.5 percent). The tapper hears the melody; the listener hears only taps.

# Why it matters for tickets

The mechanism behind [the Tapper](/smells/tapper.md), and behind experts under-sizing [Icebergs](/smells/iceberg.md). Counter it with explicit value (the "so that"), testable acceptance criteria, and shared-understanding rituals ([Three Amigos](/techniques/three-amigos.md), [Example Mapping](/techniques/example-mapping.md)).
